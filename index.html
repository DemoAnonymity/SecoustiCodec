SecoustiCodec: Streaming Speech Codec via Single-Codebook Discrete Semantic-Paralinguistic Disentanglement

Abstract

Speech codecs serve as a crucial bridge in unifying speech and text language models. Existing codec methods face several challenges in semantic encoding, such as residual paralinguistic information, insufficient semantic completeness, limited reconstruction capability, and lack of support for streaming. To address these challenges, we propose SecoustiCodec, which independently models acoustic, semantic, and paralinguistic information to achieve high-fidelity real-time streaming encoding and decoding. Firstly, to ensure semantic completeness and reconstruction fidelity, paralinguistic encoding is introduced to bridge the information gap between semantic and acoustic encoding. Secondly, a semantic-only efficient quantization method based on VAE (Variational Autoencoder) and FSQ (Finite Scalar Quantization) is proposed. This approach alleviates the long-tail distribution problem of tokens while maintaining high codebook utilization. Additionally, a semantic disentanglement method based on contrastive learning is proposed, which aligns text and speech in a joint multimodal frame-level space, effectively removing paralinguistic information from semantic encoding. Finally, a multi-stage optimization strategy is proposed to ensure robust and stable convergence. Experimental results demonstrate that SecoustiCodec outperforms mainstream codec solutions such as Encodec, FACodec, SpeechTokenizer, and MimiCodec across multiple metrics, including latency, PESQ, LSD, and MCD.

Experiment

This page shows the samples in the paper "SecoustiCodec: Streaming Speech Codec via Single-Codebook Discrete Semantic-Paralinguistic Disentanglement".

For the labeled text-speech paired data, we integrate our internal dataset with the AISHELL-3 dataset and the LibriTTS dataset, resulting in a combined total of 1,000 hours of recordings from 3,000 speakers.

All test samples and speakers have not appeared in the training set and validation set.

To ensure fairness in all objective metric evaluations, as the sampling rates of the comparison methods differ, the audio generated by SecoustiCodec and all comparison models is downsampled to 16kHz.
